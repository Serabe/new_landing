We have a Code Analaysis tool that extracts different features from the open source code available in GitHub. Our system detects language usage, number of repositories, commits... We also look for code using widely used frameworks. Currently we detect more than 150 frameworks and libraries, including Django, Flask, or Unity, and also database usage ranging including SQL and NoSQL solutions.

All these features are processed to get an overview of the professional profile of the people behind the contributions. All our information is centralized and updated every week with more repositories and the feedback from the developers we have already contacted.

Our matching algorithms use the code features extracted by our code analyzers (such as language and framework usage) to match engineers with our open job offers. The matching also takes into account an estimation of the professional experience, which we model by creating internal ranks for each language and framework we detect.

Besides the purely technical information, we also try to enrich our matching using the information publicly available in professional networks such as Linkedin. It is our intention to recommend only the most relevant job offers, and that is why we put lots of efforts in extracting relevant information such as expected roles, salary or relocation preferences.

We run our matching algorithm independently for each one of our job offers, which provides us with a list of recommended profiles. Only then we contact the suggested engineers, therefore granting that we only reach people with the right experience and background.

Our Developer Relations team answers any questions asked by the candidate. If the candidate is interested in your offer, we’ll elicit more specific information about their work experience, and how this relates to your offer.

Once these questions are answered, we set up a short call. At the end of the interview we make a judgement call on whether or not to push the candidate forward. At any stage of the process, the candidate is able to ‘step out’ if the offer does not suit them.

Following a successful interview, we prepare a report summarizing their skills and highlighting their relevant experience for the position. This report is presented to you for review. If you’d like to meet the candidate, we introduce you via email. We maintain contact with both the candidate and yourself in order to offer assistance where necessary.

If at any stage you decide to terminate a candidate’s process, we will inform them of the decision on your behalf.
