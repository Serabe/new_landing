For Developers

Introduction

What is Tyba?

Tyba is the company behind source{d}. It was started in 2011 in Madrid, Spain.

What is source{d}?

source{d} is a product by Tyba that analyses all public open-source repositories to match developers with job offers that actually suit them.

How are you different from a recruiter?

Recruiters spam you with irrelevant offers because they are not able to understand a developer profile. source{d} has been built by developers for developers to contact you only with job opportunities that match your skills and experience.

Will it cost me anything?

No, we don't charge developers a dime.

Sourcing

How did you find me?

We crawl open source contributions on Github to find out which languages and frameworks you use most. We use this data to try to identify the best developers for our open job offers. If we have contacted you, it's because we believe you are a good match for the position. We are constantly working on improving our algorithms, but sometimes we might be wrong.

How do you get my email?

Every commit you create includes the user and email you set up in your git config. When you push these commits to Github that information is kept, even though it's not directly shown on their website. We download the repositories through the Git protocol, so we can access these email addresses.

What do you know about me?

We only know the information that you have publicly shared; that is, the name and email you commit with and all your open source code. We try to complete that with your public Linkedin profile, if possible.

How can I avoid to be found?

You can go to your Github profile settings you can choose not to show your email address. This will change all your _future_ commits' email address to _yourusername_@users.noreply.github.com. As long as you keep using the same name, we will identify your new commits as belonging to you, and will assume you don't want to be contacted.

What do you do with my data?

We only use it internally for recruitment purposes. We never give it to our clients nor anyone. We started this because we hate recruiters who disrespect your inbox as much as you do, but we also hate companies who use people's data in the wrong way as much as you do.

Matching

What criteria do you use in order to match me with an offer?

We look at your open source contributions, and try to figure out which languages and frameworks you are most familiar with. Your location is also taken into account if the offer does not allow telecommuting. This is all done automatically.

We know that this method is not perfect, and we are working hard on improving it. If we've mismatched you, it means we have a bug to fix, so please don't be too harsh with us, and feel free to drop us a line; we would be more than happy to discuss any other potential offers with you.

Process

Is it legal for you to contact me?

Yes, since we only use oublic data and don't sell that data to any other parties. We started source{d} because we hated people who abuse the use they make of our data as much as you do and we wanted to do tech recruitment the right way.

How does the process look like exactly?

Once we've matched you to one of our offers we contact you to know if you'd be interested in the offer. If that would be the case we're more than happy to provide as much information as you might need and we will schedule a phone call to solve any other doubts and have a quick discussion. Some clients also ask us to do a technical test. After that, if we still consider you a good fit, we will send your contact details and a summary of your profile to our client for him to get in touch with you.
